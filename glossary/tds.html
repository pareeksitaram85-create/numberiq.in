What is TDS?

SRNumberIQ GlossaryLast reviewed June 2026

Tax Deducted at Source (TDS) is income tax withheld by the payer at the time of making specified payments, and deposited with the government on the payee's behalf.

How it works

The payer deducts at the prescribed rate (for example 10% on professional fees), deposits it by the 7th of the next month, files quarterly returns, and issues Form 16/16A. The payee claims the credit in their return.

Reference. Chapter XVII-B, Income-tax Act.

Frequently asked questions

What is TDS?

Tax withheld by the payer on specified payments and deposited with the government on the payee's behalf.

When is TDS deposited?

Generally by the 7th of the following month (for March, by 30 April).

Key takeaways

  • TDS is tax withheld at source by the payer.
  • Deposited monthly; returns filed quarterly.
  • Payee claims the credit via Form 26AS.
