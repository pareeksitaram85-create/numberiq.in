GST · Section 50

GST Interest Calculator

Interest under Section 50 of the CGST Act is 18% per annum on delayed payment of GST, and 24% per annum on wrongly availed and utilised input tax credit. It is computed on the net tax paid in cash from the day after the due date until the date of payment.

How GST interest is computed

Interest = Tax × Rate × Days ÷ 365. For GSTR-3B, the relief in Section 50(1) restricts interest to the portion of tax paid by debiting the electronic cash ledger (i.e. net of eligible ITC), provided the return is filed after the due date but tax was not already deposited.

Section 50, CGST Act 2017 — 18% p.a. on delayed tax [50(1)]; 24% p.a. on wrongly availed and utilised ITC [50(3)]. Rates notified by Notification 13/2017-Central Tax.
Tip. Interest is automatic and applies even without a notice. It is separate from the late fee under Section 47.

Frequently asked questions

Is interest charged on gross or net liability?

Per the proviso to Section 50(1), interest for delayed GSTR-3B is charged on the net cash component (tax paid through the cash ledger), not on the portion set off by ITC.

Is interest payable on ITC merely availed but reversed?

If ITC is wrongly availed but not utilised and is later reversed, interest under Section 50(3) is generally not attracted. Interest applies where the credit is both availed and utilised.

Does part of a day count?

Interest is computed on a daily basis here (days ÷ 365). Confirm the exact day-count your jurisdictional practice follows. VERIFY

Key takeaways

  • 18% p.a. on delayed tax; 24% p.a. on wrongly utilised ITC.
  • Computed from the day after due date to date of payment.
  • For GSTR-3B, charged on net cash tax (proviso to Sec 50(1)).
  • Separate from, and additional to, late fee under Section 47.
