GST registration process 2026

SRWritten by a Chartered Accountant NumberIQ Editorial Last reviewed June 2026 6 min read

GST registration is mandatory once turnover crosses Rs.40 lakh for goods or Rs.20 lakh for services (Rs.20 lakh / Rs.10 lakh in special-category states), and compulsorily in certain cases regardless of turnover. Registration is applied for in Form REG-01 with Aadhaar authentication, and the certificate (REG-06) is issued once verified.

On this pageThresholds and compulsory cases · The application process · Documents needed · Timelines and points

Thresholds and compulsory cases

TriggerThreshold / case
Goods (normal states)Rs.40 lakh aggregate turnover
Services (normal states)Rs.20 lakh
Special-category statesRs.20 lakh (goods) / Rs.10 lakh (services)
Inter-state supply of goodsCompulsory (Section 24)
E-commerce operators / suppliersCompulsory
Reverse-charge liability, casual/NR taxable personCompulsory

VERIFY the threshold for your state and supply type.

The application process

  1. File Form REG-01 Part A (PAN, mobile, email) — get a TRN.
  2. Complete Part B with business details, place of business, bank, and authorised signatory.
  3. Complete Aadhaar authentication; many applicants now undergo biometric authentication at a GST Suvidha Kendra.
  4. Upload documents; the officer verifies (and may seek clarification in REG-03).
  5. The GSTIN and certificate REG-06 are issued.

Documents needed

  • PAN and Aadhaar of the proprietor/partners/directors.
  • Proof of place of business (electricity bill, rent agreement, NOC).
  • Bank account proof (cancelled cheque / statement).
  • Photographs and the constitution document (partnership deed, COI).

Timelines and points

  • Approval is typically within 7 working days (longer where biometric/physical verification applies).
  • Aadhaar-authenticated, low-risk applicants are processed faster.
  • Register within 30 days of becoming liable.
  • Use the GSTIN verification after registration.

Frequently asked questions

What is the turnover limit for GST registration?

Rs.40 lakh for goods and Rs.20 lakh for services in normal states (Rs.20 lakh / Rs.10 lakh in special-category states); some cases require registration regardless of turnover.

Is GST registration compulsory for inter-state sales?

Yes, inter-state supply of goods requires registration regardless of turnover under Section 24, along with e-commerce, casual, non-resident and reverse-charge cases.

How long does GST registration take?

Usually within about 7 working days for Aadhaar-authenticated applicants; longer where biometric or physical verification is required.

Key takeaways

  • Thresholds: Rs.40 lakh goods / Rs.20 lakh services (lower in special states).
  • Inter-state, e-commerce, RCM and casual cases: compulsory.
  • Apply via REG-01 with Aadhaar/biometric authentication.
  • Certificate REG-06 issued, typically within ~7 working days.
