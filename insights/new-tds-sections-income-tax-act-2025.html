TDS / TCS

New TDS sections under the Income-tax Act 2025

SRWritten by a Chartered Accountant NumberIQ Editorial Last reviewed June 2026 5 min read

From FY 2026-27, the Income-tax Act 2025 renumbers TDS: salary TDS moves to Section 392, non-salary TDS provisions consolidate into Section 393, and TCS moves to Section 394. The rates and thresholds broadly continue; the section references and forms change, so deductors must remap their systems.

On this pageThe high-level mapping · What stays the same · What changes · Transition checklist

The high-level mapping

Old Act (1961)New Act (2025)
192 (salary), 192A (EPF)392
193-196 and 194-series (non-salary)393 (consolidated, with sub-clauses)
206C (TCS)394

The non-salary payments (194C, 194J, 194I, 194H, 194A, 195, etc.) are reorganised as sub-clauses within Section 393. ✓ Verified June 2026 for each specific payment against the enacted text.

What stays the same

  • The rates and thresholds broadly continue from the 1961 framework (as amended by recent Finance Acts).
  • The mechanics — deduct at credit/payment, deposit, quarterly returns — continue.
  • Higher-rate rules for no-PAN/non-filers carry over.

What changes

  • Every section reference in agreements, letters, certificates and software.
  • Possibly the return and certificate form numbers — confirm the notified forms.
  • The single 'tax year' terminology replaces previous year/assessment year.

Transition checklist

  1. Build a 1961-to-2025 concordance for the sections you use.
  2. Update ERP/payroll TDS masters to the new sections.
  3. Refresh templates and SOPs with new references.
  4. Track the latest CBDT notifications during the transition.

Frequently asked questions

What replaces Section 192 under the new Act?

Salary TDS (and EPF withdrawal under 192A) moves to Section 392 of the Income-tax Act 2025.

Where do the 194-series TDS sections go?

They consolidate into Section 393, reorganised as sub-clauses for each type of non-salary payment.

Do TDS rates change under the new Act?

Broadly no — the renumbering is structural; rates and thresholds continue as amended by recent Finance Acts. Confirm specifics against the enacted text.

Key takeaways

  • From FY 2026-27: salary 392, non-salary 393, TCS 394.
  • Non-salary 194-series become sub-clauses of Section 393.
  • Rates and mechanics broadly continue; references change.
  • Remap agreements, software and templates during transition.
