How to verify a GSTIN online

SRWritten by a Chartered Accountant NumberIQ Editorial Last reviewed June 2026 4 min read

You can verify any GSTIN free on the GST portal using the 'Search Taxpayer' tool, which shows the legal name, registration status, type and date. Verifying a vendor's GSTIN and return-filing history before transacting protects your input tax credit, since ITC depends on the supplier's compliance.

On this pageThe 15-digit GSTIN structure · How to verify · Why it matters for ITC · Practical points

The 15-digit GSTIN structure

DigitsMeaning
1-2State code
3-12PAN of the taxpayer
13Entity number (registrations on the same PAN in the state)
14Default 'Z'
15Checksum character

A GSTIN that does not fit this 15-character pattern, or whose embedded PAN does not match the vendor's PAN, is a red flag.

How to verify

  1. Go to the GST portal → Search Taxpayer → 'Search by GSTIN/UIN'.
  2. Enter the GSTIN; view the legal/trade name, status, registration date and type.
  3. Check 'Show Filing Table' for the return-filing history.
  4. Confirm the status is Active (not cancelled/suspended).

Why it matters for ITC

Your input tax credit depends on the supplier filing their returns and paying tax. A vendor whose registration is cancelled, or who consistently fails to file GSTR-1/3B, puts your ITC at risk (Section 16(2)(c)). A periodic GSTIN check builds the bona fide-buyer defence against retrospective cancellation.

Practical points

  • Verify new vendors at onboarding and high-risk vendors periodically.
  • Save a screenshot of the status at the transaction date.
  • Match the GSTIN's PAN with the vendor's PAN.
  • Cross-check the invoice GSTIN against the master before claiming ITC.

Frequently asked questions

How do I check if a GSTIN is valid?

Use the 'Search Taxpayer' tool on the GST portal — enter the GSTIN to see the legal name, status, type and filing history; an active status confirms validity.

What does the GSTIN structure tell me?

The first two digits are the state code, digits 3-12 are the PAN, digit 13 is the entity number, digit 14 is 'Z', and digit 15 is a checksum.

Why verify a vendor's GSTIN?

Because your input tax credit depends on the supplier's compliance; verifying status and filing history protects your ITC and supports a bona fide-buyer defence.

Key takeaways

  • Verify free via 'Search Taxpayer' on the GST portal.
  • GSTIN = state(2) + PAN(10) + entity(1) + Z + checksum.
  • Confirm status is Active and check filing history.
  • Protects ITC and builds the bona fide-buyer defence.
