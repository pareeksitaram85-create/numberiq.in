Financial ratios for CA client monitoring

SRWritten by a Chartered Accountant NumberIQ Editorial Last reviewed June 2026 6 min read

For client monitoring, track four families of ratios: liquidity (current, quick), leverage (debt-equity, interest coverage), profitability (gross/net margin, ROCE) and efficiency (debtor, creditor and inventory days). Together they reveal short-term solvency, capital structure risk, earning quality and working-capital health.

On this pageThe four families · How to compute the key ones · Reading them together · Build it into monitoring

The four families

FamilyKey ratiosSignals
LiquidityCurrent ratio, quick ratioAbility to meet short-term dues
LeverageDebt-equity, interest coverageCapital-structure risk
ProfitabilityGross/net margin, ROCE, ROEEarning power and returns
EfficiencyDebtor days, creditor days, inventory daysWorking-capital management

How to compute the key ones

  • Current ratio = current assets ÷ current liabilities (healthy ~1.5-2).
  • Quick ratio = (current assets − inventory) ÷ current liabilities (~1).
  • Debt-equity = total debt ÷ equity (lower is safer; sector-dependent).
  • Interest coverage = EBIT ÷ interest (comfortably > 2-3).
  • DSO = (debtors ÷ credit sales) × 365.

Reading them together

No ratio is meaningful alone. Falling margins with rising debtor days may signal aggressive sales on poor credit terms; a high current ratio built on slow inventory is not real liquidity. Compare against prior periods, budget and industry benchmarks, and read the trend, not a single point.

Build it into monitoring

  • Automate the ratios from the trial balance in your monthly MIS.
  • Set thresholds/alerts for ratios breaching a band.
  • Track the cash conversion cycle (DSO + inventory days − DPO).
  • Use trends to start the client conversation early.

Frequently asked questions

Which financial ratios matter most for monitoring?

Liquidity (current, quick), leverage (debt-equity, interest coverage), profitability (margins, ROCE) and efficiency (debtor, creditor and inventory days) together give a rounded picture.

What is a healthy current ratio?

Generally around 1.5 to 2, though the ideal depends on the industry; very high ratios may indicate idle assets or slow-moving inventory.

How do I use ratios for early warning?

Track them monthly against prior periods, budget and benchmarks, set alert thresholds, and watch trends — deteriorating ratios flag issues before they hit the cash flow.

Key takeaways

  • Four families: liquidity, leverage, profitability, efficiency.
  • Compute consistently; read against prior periods and benchmarks.
  • Watch the cash conversion cycle (DSO + inventory days - DPO).
  • Automate in the MIS with threshold alerts for early warning.
