TDS return filing: 24Q, 26Q, 27Q

SRWritten by a Chartered Accountant NumberIQ Editorial Last reviewed June 2026 6 min read

TDS returns are filed quarterly: Form 24Q for salary, Form 26Q for resident non-salary payments, Form 27Q for payments to non-residents and Form 27EQ for TCS. Returns are due by 31 July, 31 October, 31 January and 31 May for the four quarters, with a late fee of Rs.200 per day under Section 234E.

Which form for which payment

FormCovers
24QTDS on salary (Section 192)
26QTDS on resident non-salary payments (194C, 194J, 194I, etc.)
27QTDS on payments to non-residents (Section 195)
27EQTCS (Section 206C)

Due dates

QuarterPeriodDue date
Q1Apr-Jun31 July
Q2Jul-Sep31 October
Q3Oct-Dec31 January
Q4Jan-Mar31 May

Late fee and penalty

  • Section 234E — late-filing fee of Rs.200 per day until the return is filed, capped at the TDS amount. This fee cannot be waived.
  • Section 271H — penalty of Rs.10,000 to Rs.1,00,000 for non-filing or incorrect filing.
  • Form 16 (salary) follows 24Q; Form 16A follows 26Q.

Practical points

  • Validate PANs before filing to avoid short-deduction defaults.
  • File even nil returns where applicable to avoid notices.
  • Correct errors through a correction statement.
  • Reconcile the return to challans and Form 26AS of payees.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between 24Q, 26Q and 27Q?

24Q is for salary TDS, 26Q for resident non-salary payments, 27Q for payments to non-residents, and 27EQ for TCS.

What are the TDS return due dates?

31 July, 31 October, 31 January and 31 May for quarters 1 to 4 respectively.

What is the late fee for a TDS return?

Rs.200 per day under Section 234E until filed (capped at the TDS amount), plus a possible penalty of Rs.10,000-1,00,000 under Section 271H.

Key takeaways

  • 24Q salary, 26Q resident non-salary, 27Q non-resident, 27EQ TCS.
  • Due 31 Jul / 31 Oct / 31 Jan / 31 May.
  • Section 234E late fee Rs.200/day (cannot be waived).
  • Issue Form 16 (24Q) and Form 16A (26Q) after filing.
