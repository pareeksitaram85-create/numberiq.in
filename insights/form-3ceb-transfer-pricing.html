Form 3CEB for transfer pricing

SRWritten by a Chartered Accountant NumberIQ Editorial Last reviewed June 2026 5 min read

Form 3CEB is the accountant's report under Section 92E certifying the particulars of international transactions and specified domestic transactions entered into with associated enterprises. It must be obtained from a Chartered Accountant and filed by 31 October of the assessment year, independent of whether a TP adjustment arises.

On this pageWhat Form 3CEB is · What it captures · Deadline and penalty · Practical points

What Form 3CEB is

Form 3CEB is a mandatory report by a Chartered Accountant that lists and verifies each international transaction (and specified domestic transaction) with associated enterprises, the method applied, and the arm's length position. It is required even if the transactions are small or already at arm's length — the existence of such a transaction triggers the filing.

What it captures

  • Details of the taxpayer and its associated enterprises.
  • Nature and value of each international/specified domestic transaction.
  • The most appropriate method applied to each.
  • Whether the transaction is at arm's length.

Deadline and penalty

ItemPosition
Due date of Form 3CEB31 October of the assessment year
Return due date (TP cases)30 November
Penalty for non-filing (Section 271BA)Rs.1,00,000
Penalty for non-maintenance of documents (271AA)2% of transaction value

Practical points

  • 3CEB is filed before the return; the return for TP cases is due 30 November.
  • The CA signing 3CEB must independently examine the benchmarking and documentation.
  • Even a single share allotment or loan to a foreign AE can trigger 3CEB.
  • Keep the Rule 10D documentation ready to support each disclosure.

Frequently asked questions

Who has to file Form 3CEB?

Any taxpayer that has entered into an international transaction or a specified domestic transaction with an associated enterprise during the year, regardless of the transaction value.

What is the due date for Form 3CEB?

31 October of the assessment year; the income-tax return for taxpayers with such transactions is then due by 30 November.

What is the penalty for not filing 3CEB?

Rs.1,00,000 under Section 271BA, in addition to a 2% penalty on transaction value under Section 271AA for failure to maintain or furnish documentation.

Key takeaways

  • 3CEB is the CA's report on AE transactions under Section 92E.
  • Required whenever an AE transaction exists, however small.
  • Due 31 October; TP-case return due 30 November.
  • Non-filing penalty Rs.1,00,000 (Section 271BA).
