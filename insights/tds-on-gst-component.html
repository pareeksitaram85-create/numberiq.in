TDS on the GST component of an invoice

SRWritten by a Chartered Accountant NumberIQ Editorial Last reviewed June 2026 4 min read

Per CBDT Circular 23/2017, income-tax TDS is deducted on the amount paid or payable excluding the GST component, provided the GST is indicated separately on the invoice. If GST is not shown separately, TDS is deducted on the whole (gross) amount.

On this pageThe rule · The condition and a caution · Worked example · Practical points

The rule

CBDT Circular 23/2017 clarified that where, under the contract or invoice, the GST on services is shown separately, TDS under Chapter XVII-B is deducted on the amount excluding that GST. This avoids deducting tax on tax. The clarification applies across TDS sections (194C, 194J, 194I, etc.).

The condition and a caution

The relief applies only when GST is separately indicated. If the invoice shows a single consolidated amount without splitting GST, TDS is deducted on the gross amount. Note the circular refers to GST on services; for composite supplies, ensure the GST is clearly bifurcated.

Worked example

Professional fee Rs.1,00,000 + GST 18% Rs.18,000 = Rs.1,18,000, with GST shown separately. TDS under 194J at 10% = Rs.10,000 (on Rs.1,00,000), not Rs.11,800. The payee receives Rs.1,08,000 and the GST is paid in full.

Practical points

  • Always split GST on invoices to your customers to enable correct TDS.
  • The same logic applies to TDS under GST (Section 51) on the taxable value.
  • Document the bifurcation for audit.

Frequently asked questions

Is TDS deducted on the GST amount?

No, provided GST is shown separately on the invoice. Per CBDT Circular 23/2017, TDS is deducted on the amount excluding the GST component.

What if GST is not shown separately?

Then TDS is deducted on the gross (GST-inclusive) amount, as the relief in Circular 23/2017 requires GST to be separately indicated.

Does this apply to all TDS sections?

Yes, the principle applies across Chapter XVII-B TDS sections such as 194C, 194J and 194I where GST on services is shown separately.

Key takeaways

  • TDS is on the value excluding separately-shown GST (Circular 23/2017).
  • If GST is not split, TDS is on the gross amount.
  • Applies across 194C/194J/194I and similar sections.
  • Always bifurcate GST on invoices for correct TDS.
