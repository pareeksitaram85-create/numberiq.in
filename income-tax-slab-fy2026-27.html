Income Tax Slab FY 2026-27

Under the new regime for FY 2026-27, income up to Rs.4 lakh is nil-rated, rising to 30% above Rs.24 lakh, with a Section 87A rebate making income up to Rs.12 lakh tax-free for residents. The old regime retains the Rs.2.5 lakh exemption with deductions. Verify against the Finance Act 2026.

Frequently asked questions

Is income up to Rs.12 lakh tax-free under the new regime?

For residents, yes — the Section 87A rebate makes tax nil up to Rs.12 lakh of taxable income (Rs.12.75 lakh for salaried after the Rs.75,000 standard deduction), with marginal relief above.

What is the standard deduction for FY 2026-27?

Rs.75,000 under the new regime and Rs.50,000 under the old regime, for salaried individuals and pensioners.

Which regime is the default?

The new regime is the default; the old regime must be specifically opted for, and business-income taxpayers use Form 10-IEA.
