Section 49(5) — Mandatory Set-Off Rules

  • 1
    IGST Credit → First against IGST output liability, then against CGST, then against SGST/UTGST.
    The IGST credit must be fully exhausted before CGST/SGST credits can be used to pay IGST [Proviso to Sec 49(5)(a)].
  • 2
    CGST Credit → First against CGST output liability, then against IGST.
    Cannot be used to pay SGST/UTGST. [Section 49(5)(b)]
  • 3
    SGST/UTGST Credit → First against SGST/UTGST output liability, then against IGST.
    Cannot be used to pay CGST. [Section 49(5)(c)]
  • 4
    Cash → Any remaining output liability after exhausting all eligible ITC must be paid via the Electronic Cash Ledger. [Section 49(1)]
Key amendment — CGST Amendment Act 2018 (w.e.f. 1 Feb 2019): Before the amendment, taxpayers had discretion in cross-utilisation. The amendment made the sequence mandatory and added the proviso requiring IGST credit to be fully utilised before any other credit is used to pay IGST. Circular No. 98/17/2019-GST dated 23 April 2019 clarified the sequence.
